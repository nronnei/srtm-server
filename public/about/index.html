About the Project

What are we doing?

Uncertainty is a fact of life in science. In spatial science it is a particularly significant challenge because it permeates the data, analysis, and results of every applied and scientific project. Our work aims to make researchers around the world aware of uncertainty in their work and assist them in developing uncertainty-aware processing chains and more meaningful results.

The Problem

Error and uncertainty are inherent to global terrain datasets like SRTM and GDEM and more broadly, all DEMs. It is absolutely essential to account for this uncertainty in GIS operations that rely on this data because uncertainty propagates to any derived products. This propagation can have very serious consequences such as the potential invalidation of model results. Geostatistical methods have between developed to mitigate this problem, but they require expert knowledge to apply them to a project. Despite the fact that it has been discussed in the literature for over two decades, there has been very little progress in making uncertainty propagation analysis accessible to those who are not geostatistics experts - the majority of GIS users.
